Certificates

Certificates mark evidenced learning milestones. They do not claim academic accreditation, ecclesiastical approval or professional qualification.

Principle: a certificate should reflect completed study, active work and an approved completion test—not simply viewing pages or videos.

Course milestones

Individual Experiments may define clearly labelled completion milestones.

Evidence first

Notebook work, recall, correction and review must support any completion claim.

Honest limits

Every certificate must state what it does—and does not—represent.
